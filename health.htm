Mpox Outbreak Update in Malawi

The Public Health Institute of Malawi (PHIM) reported 8 new Mpox cases in Lilongwe District...

As of September 13, 2025, PHIM confirmed 110 total Mpox cases since the outbreak began on April 17, 2025. Eight new patients from Lilongwe were treated in various hospitals including Bwaila, Milamba, Dzenza, and St. Chavala. Symptoms included fever, fatigue, headaches, body aches, and rash. PHIM has implemented a One-Health approach to contain the outbreak. 47 patients have recovered, 14 remain under clinical care, and two were lost-to-follow-up. Patients are in both hospital and home isolation across Lilongwe, Mzimba South, Ntchisi, and Karonga.

Extensive Contact Tracing for Mpox

Rapid response teams traced 839 contacts, most have completed monitoring without symptoms...

Out of 839 contacts traced, 830 completed their 21-day monitoring period without developing symptoms. Six contacts are still under active monitoring. Five of the contacts later tested positive for Mpox. The Ministry of Health implemented a comprehensive strategy including laboratory PCR testing, genomic sequencing, healthcare training, and community engagement in affected areas. Essential medicines and PPE have been distributed, and public health messages in Chichewa, Tumbuka, Swahili, and English are being disseminated widely.
